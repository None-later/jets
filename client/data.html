How the FT compiled a database on aircraft perquisites at the biggest US-listed companies

The Financial Times has published an article opening a window onto the world of corporate jets perquisites...

About the data

To compile this database on executives’ personal use of company planes, the FT manually inspected nearly a thousand company filings to the Securities and Exchanges Commission. We looked at annual proxy filings (also known as DEF 14A filings) of S&P 500 companies across two financial years (FY2013 and FY2014).

Where a DEF 14A was not available (for example due to mergers and acquisitions activity), we looked for other proxy statements filed by the company that contained the information, such as PRE 14A statements.

To ease the process of finding all the filings, we used the SEC’s File Transfer Protocol (FTP) server for its EDGAR electronic filing system to get and compile an index of the filings we were looking for.

For our analysis, we included only the 465 companies that were in the S&P 500 from January 1, 2014 to December 2015 (the period during which the proxy statements we examined were filed). Since SEC regulations do not require companies to disclose the value of personal use of corporate aircraft by a named executive if it is less than $25,000, we discarded any voluntary disclosure of aircraft perquisites that were below that threshold.

This left us with 322 executives across 184 companies.

We entered the data into a Google spreadsheet, and then used R, the statistical programming language, to conduct our analysis.

Footnotes

  • There is no data available for Mylan in 2014 because the company did not file a proxy statement with that information that year.
  • The amounts quoted for Humana and Duke Energy overstate the value of aircraft perquisites given to their executives when compared to other companies in our database. Both Humana and Duke Energy included, in their reported figure, the value of disallowed corporate income tax deductions. Other companies either explicitly excluded that amount, or did not make clear whether that cost was included. Humana and Duke Energy said they included that amount, but did not break out the value of it, so we were not able to exclude that from our calculations.
  • The amount quoted for CBS executives overstate the value of aircraft perquisites, since the figure reported by CBS is for "transportation-related benefits" and includes the cost for a personal car and driver. Other companies report car and airplane perquities separately.
